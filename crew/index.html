(Writer/ Director / Producer/ Editor)

Naomi is a writer/ director based in Ithaca, NY. She has a B.S. in Cinema and Photography from Ithaca College and a M.A. in Screenwriting from the National Film School of Ireland. Naomi is currently heading up the writing department at Let's Create a Game as well as teaching community screenwriting classes and running the Screenwriter's Guild of Ithaca.

(Producer)

Charmed by Will Smith in The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air, Danielle knew she was destined to work in Television at a young age and made that dream come true by immediately moving to L.A. after graduating from Ithaca College with a degree in Cinema & Photography and minors in Business Administration and Sociology. Danielle landed atImagine Entertainment, an A-List production company. After a year working on some of the best shows on television (24, Friday Night Lights, Parenthood), Danielle moved over to NBCUniversal where she is a Coordinator in Current Programming and Drama Development.

(Director of Photography)

Kyle has worked on a wide variety of projects for companies including Sesame Street, Insignia Films, Funny Or Die, and NYU, among others. He just finished shooting 2nd camera on a major PBS series about the US Constitution, and is in production as Director of Photography on the feature film, Cocks of the Walk. Kyle is also Co-Director and D.P. on They Might Be Giants, a feature documentary film. Worlds We Created, a short he filmed and co-produced this past summer, is currently in the festival circuit. Kyle currently shoots for Bull Moose Pictures.

(Lighting Director/ Co-Producer)

Kate received her B.S. in Cinema, Photography and B.A. in Art from Ithaca College. She currently does freelance graphic design, marketing and PR. Kate also works with Northeast Paranormal Investigations Society and has a fun food blog.

(On-set Technical Supervisor/ Post-production Technical Specialist)

(set design/ production assistant)

When Christine was a child she was asked every year to write an essay on what she wanted to be when she grew up. She alternated between wanting to be a scientist and an artist. One year she said that she wanted to be both and was told by the teacher to pick one. Her first career was as a research biologist in plant pathology. Presently she is developing her skills in photography and the visual arts. Who says that you can't do both?
