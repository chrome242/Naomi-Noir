Teaching technique: Half of the class will be spent teaching new material,
    the other half will be used for writing exercises and work-shopping homework
    assignments.

    Course goals: gain general knowledge of screenwriting as well as learn
    techniques to writing a concise, well-structured, and compelling script. At
    the end of the course students will have written a variety of short
    exercises and short 5-10 page script.

    Homework: homework is optional, but essential if you wish to have a finished
    short script by the end of the 8 week course. All assignments can be e-
    mailed in Final Draft or Pdf format by Sunday evening to contact@naominoir.
    com for feedback by Tuesday’s class.


    Week 1: Intro to Screenwriting

    Theme, genre, coming up with a screenplay idea that will sustain momentum
    through Second Act, formatting script: descriptions/ action/ dialogue,
    screenwriting software, general overview of course

    Homework: paragraph summary of two script ideas
            

    Week 2: Plot + Structure

    Plot vs. character as ways to approach writing a script, short vs. feature-
    length scripts, Three Act Structure (plot points), non-traditional
    structures, central conflict

    Homework: Three-act structure for your film idea


    Week 3: Character

    Character tree (traits/ history), character arcs, wants vs. needs,
    Enneagram, how to get audience to identify with character

    Homework: Character Tree of main character in your script


    Week 4: Outlines

    Beat-sheet, Treatment, Sequences (Frank Daniel)

    Homework: Write beat sheet divided into sequences for your script


    Week 5: Visual Story-telling

    Telling story through action/ behavior rather than dialogue, visual
    descriptions  (as if/ like…), Scene of Introduction

    Homework: Write a ‘Scene of Introduction’ for a character in your script
    without using dialogue


    Week 6: Dialogue

    Finding your character’s voice, how to avoid on-the-nose dialogue, words vs.
    action (or how to let your character lie)

    Homework: start writing short script


    Week 7: Scene Writing

    Brief overview of the different types of scenes, Power Struggle Scenes vs.
    Scenes of Negotiation, Scenes of Decision

    Homework: e-mail copy of short script for feedback


    Week 6: Selling your Script + the Second Draft

    Loglines, the pitch + tips for editing, techniques for overcoming writer’s
    block

    Homework: start your second draft, write a treatment, outline your next
    film, write!
